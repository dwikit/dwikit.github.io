About Me

My name is David Wikstrom, and I’m an aspiring Virtual Reality developer based out of Seattle, Washington. Virtual Reality has been something that has fascinated me since I was very young, and due to somewhat recent developments in my life I now have the opportunity, skills, and determination to build the virtual worlds and experiences that have always fascinated me.

I have recently graduated from Bellevue College in Washington State with a Bachelor’s Degree in Application Development. I will use the coding skills and foundations that I have gained throughout my time going back to school to further sharpen and increase my skill set to pursue my goals. I wish to use the skills that I have gained to build XR applications in order to help people. Through the development of medical, therapeutic, educational, skill-building, storytelling, and experiences that bring the users’ joy.

A quick breakdown of my skills and the technologies I have experience working with can be found here: Skills.

more about me

The following sections will briefly cover my personal growth and experiences from my childhood, time living in Japan, my military experience, going back to Bellevue College for my bachelor’s degree, and my married life.


Childhood

I’ve loved the concept of virtual worlds ever since I played my first video game, Pole Position, on the Atari 2600. This fascination continued to grow and develop as I was exposed to one of my favorite movies, Tron. Through this mind-boggling 1982 perfection, my imagination was ignited with the idea of being pulled into other dimensions and experiences that could transform reality. My curiosity continued to be fueled with the release of The Matrix and with escaping through various video games and immersive story experiences throughout the years.

Japan

My time in Japan may not be directly connected to my interest in XR, but experiencing another vastly different culture from my own humbled me and opened my eyes to locational realities within our world. It taught me to appreciate and explore a life that is distinct and unique, showing me that until that point, I had only known one reality, my own. Through my time in Japan, I was privileged to learn enough of the language, enabling me to form deep relationships and explore the culture through food, music, and art.


Military

During my teen years, I spent my summers working at fisheries in Alaska putting in over 100 hours a week, so the value of hard work was a concept instilled in me very early in life. This followed me as I enlisted with the United States Air Force as an Electrical and Environmental Systems Specialist, which offered me the opportunity to gain a new set of skills through working with complex military systems. I gained a deep understanding of troubleshooting and completing tasks in high-pressure situations with little to no room for error. As I rose in rank, I achieved leadership positions, training my superiors and younger airmen, which solidified me as a highly valued specialist in my field. In doing so, I gained teamwork and leadership experience along with an understanding of how to manage teams during stressful situations.


Bellevue College

While my time in the Air Force was invaluable, it pushed my body to the limits resulting in a disability and a medical discharge from the Air Force. With my career at a crossroads, I had a decision to make, wallow in self-pity and loss of purpose or find a new purpose and move forth with my life. I chose the latter and started exploring different paths. While I have always been passionate about Virtual Reality, I also want to help people. Still, most positions require some type of formal degree, so I ultimately decided to enroll in Bellevue College to pursue my bachelor’s degree. The time in which I enrolled into the college was also the first quarter that the College was offering its first-ever accredited Virtual Reality course. It was fate, and I signed up immediately.

Within this class, we had to choose either to write a research paper or create a virtual reality application. I chose the much harder path of creating a virtual reality application, with having no experience with coding or creating applications of any kind. I took it as a challenge and taught myself the basics of coding in C# and Unity. The creation of my first object and interaction with that object in a virtual world brought me back to the same feeling I had as a child playing on the Atari 2600. I had found my new purpose. Since then, I changed my major to Application Development, and over the course of the last 3 years, I have dedicated my studies to learn about the creation of Virtual Reality applications. Over these years, I've gained skills and knowledge from completing projects and utilizing various programming languages, frameworks, databases, and libraries through game engines such as Unity and Unreal and web development.

While at Bellevue College, I also had the opportunity to volunteer my time with the VR Club as their vice president for 3 years. This allowed me to spread more awareness about XR and introduce the technology to new students as they joined Bellevue College.

I was also employed with Bellevue College’s XR Lab as a senior lab assistant and worked with a team to get this technology into the hands of students using equitable practices. Through research into available applications, we augmented teachers’ classrooms by providing workshops and creating virtual reality curriculum for their courses.

Tofu is scared of everything including his own shadow, and Bean loves everything but also tries to fight everything. Proposal

This project was put forth and proposed by Bellevue College's Medical Sciences Division in collaboration with the XR Lab. My team was assigned to the project, and we worked with our client, a cardiologist of over 40 years, and the Ultrasound Lab professor at the college.

Team

Our team was an Agile SCRUM team, of which I was the Product Owner. We also had a SCRUM Master, two team members on the development team, and a data analyst. As our team was very small the Product Owner and SCRUM Master were also considered part of the develepment team. The original project proposal was to use ultrasound datasets and build a system to visualize the human heart using those datasets in a 3D virtual environment.

Scope

The project ended up shifting dramatically from this initial proposal as more information was gathered through investigation. Our client wanted a more open, scalable teaching tool that taught users about human anatomy in VR, and he wanted an easy tool to use that provided information on human anatomy. From these gathered requirements, feasibility and design stages took place, and the VR Anatomy Viewer was born.

Our product scope for this project was to have an intractable heart that had a label system, could be scaled up, made transparent, and have controllable animation and audio. Through further testing and prototyping, we found also needed a way to rotate the heart. We also needed an environment and lighting, and this was our initial scope. Contracts with the client were then finalized, and our team had a little less than 3 months left to complete the project.

Prototypying and Testing

We prototyped and tested out different interactions and assets in a test project and then brought them over to the main project after testing.

Documentation, Communication, and Version Control

We used Trello for our Agile board, task tracking, and visual progress. We also used SharePoint for documentation, Discord for communication, and GitHub, as well as Unity Collaborate for version control.

Open VR

As this is a Bellevue College application, our team needed to target the VR devices that the college's XR Lab had available. We decided to use SteamVR and the OpenVR API to support all of the devices that we needed to target.

Development

As the Product Owner, I set up our meetings, relayed information from our client to the rest of the team, and built out our team's product backlog. Also, because I was part of the development team as well, I worked on any and every task that I could get my hands on. Our team worked in one-week sprints to complete our tasks. We would refine our backlog, have a Sprint Planning meeting at the start of each week, daily check-ins on discord, have a Sprint Review meeting at the end of the week, and hold a Sprint Retrospective meeting afterward.

Challenges

During the course of development, our team ran into many challenges that we would eventually overcome. The difficulties presented by COVID-19, personal challenges for each team member, standard issues of blockers on certain tasks, merge conflicts, GitHub file size restrictions, time, etc. As a team, we overcame these challenges together and supported each other through difficult times.

Project Completion

Our team completed the project's scope within the allotted timeframe, and we presented the completed application to our client and Bellevue College's Medical Sciences Division. They were thrilled at how the project turned out and are excited to build on it with future capstone teams. The project was a great success for my team, our client, the XR Lab, and Bellevue College, and I am excited to see where the future leads.

VR Music Cubes

As this was the first project I had ever attempted to create using a 3D engine like Unity and I hadn't coded anything before, I needed a plan.

  1. Gain a basic understanding of Unity.
  2. Gain knowledge of C#.
  3. Gain a basic understanding of audio structure.
  4. Prototype and create a proof of concept using knowledge gained.
  5. Add VR support.
  6. Research how to create a compelling 3D environment.
  7. Refine the user's gameplay experience.
  8. Gather feedback and make adjustments as needed.
  9. Put finishing touches in and make any needed visual changes.
  10. Deliver completed experience by project deadline.

First script completed!

After a lot of research, trial, and error, and getting familiar with Unity. The audio data was now being captured, and I got the first audio script working!

Now it was time to use that data to manipulate an object, and utilizing an asset called Visualizer Studio helped me accomplish that goal.

Project concept

Now that an object was reacting to audio, I needed to conceptualize the user experience further. What would the user be doing in this application? After some deliberation, I decided to have the user experience be more of a chill interactive experience. An experience where the user would not be overwhelmed by many visual elements but let the user focus on a few compelling objects within the environment.

Needed features

  • Objects to represent audio highs, mids, and lows
  • VR integration
  • User abilities: force push and force pull
  • Respawn system for cubes
  • Needed a sky for the environment
  • Post-Processing effects to make the environment and objects visually appealing

Build and challenges

Within the 3 month timeframe, all planned features were completed by the project deadline. Many lessons were learned throughout the project, and many blockers were overcome.


Project Completion

A few realizations were made throughout the project; I loved the challenge of jumping into the unknown and building something compelling. I also realized that this is what I want to do for the future, so I changed my major to gain the coding foundations necessary to create compelling experiences.

BC IBIT Capstone VR Class

For this time-sensitive project, we had about a week and a half to get an entire capstone class into virtual reality before the end of the quarter. As a team, we devised a plan, split up our tasks, and researched various VR applications that would potentially be a good fit for the class in VR. We landed on Rec Room as the VR application that would best suit our needs as the social room capacity in Rec Room exceeded the class capacity of 17 students. RecRoom also has some amazing environmental editing tools that allowed us to build the environment to have different themed areas for each capstone team's project and a main area for the whole class.

We then built out the environment, hand delivered VR headsets to each student, and provided recorded instruction for the entire class.


The VR class was a major success for the XR Lab. It was the first of its kind for the college and possibly the State of Washington, and I'm proud to have played a major role in every stage of the project.
